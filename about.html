Background and Significance of the Study

The value of composting to communities and the environment is that the use of chemicals will be lessened and the pile of smelly biodegradable trash will be used in a good way that can be so helpful to the environment. With the help of composting plants will be able to grow better since commercial fertilizers has a lot of toxic chemicals in it which can make the growth of the plant weaker, not only that composting will be using the biodegradable waste you use everyday meaning you will not need to buy really expensive fertilizers that might not be even good for your plants.The specific institution I am engaged in will benefit from composting because not only it will make their surroundings be more healthier and green, composting can also be a way for abandoned and abused kids to get their mind of what happened to them or maybe do it as a hobby.

STATEMENT OF THE PROBLEM:

How will this compost affect the growth of radish?

HYPOTHESIS:

If we use grass clippings, eggshells and banana peels as fertilizer, then the radish will grow fast

MATERIALS AND EQUIPMENT NEEDED

Banana Peel

50g/compost starting material/Cavendish Banana

Bone Meal

50g/compost starting material/Meat Bone Waste

Grass Clippings

50g/compost starting material/yard grass

Egg Shells

50g/compost starting material/1 week old seedlings

Procedure For Testing

Producing or Sourcing the Compost Material


Step 1
: Buy or Gather all materials needed for the compost (Radish Seedling, Loam Soil)
Step 2:
Prepare the following independent variables for the compost (banana peels, grass clipping and egg shells) Eggshells will be gathered from leftover used eggs after let them dry out for a few days or place them in a warm oven to dry more quickly.(Drying a shell allows them to crush more completely before we add them to our compost bin) Banana Peels will be gathered from leftover peels, simply cut the bananas and/or their peels using a kitchen knife into small pieces, the largest being two inches. Grass Cliipping Add 6-10 inches of fine plant material, such as the fresh grass clipping from the yard
Step 3:
Once all of the following materials are ready for compost put them aside till the test plant is ready

Sourcing and Preparing the Test Plants and Plant Soile


Step 1
: Mix it all together Now lets mix the compost, First, add 50g of dried banana peels, 50 grams of grass clippings, and 50g smashed eggshells to make the fertilizer, then, mix all the components into the 350g of Loam soil.
Step 2
: Plant the seeds Next, plant the seed in the pot 1-inch deep and pour 30ml of water daily. when the test subject is maturing increase the volume of water into 100 ml once in 3 days.
Step 3
: Label the pots Label the pot A, and B as Experimental with corresponding numbers 1, and 2 and pot C and D as controlled as with the corresponding number 1 and 2.
Step 4
: Placement of the pots Place the pot A, and C(Experimental-1,Controlled-2,) in a sunny area for at least 6 hours. Place the pot B and D (Experimental-2, Controlled-1) in a sunny area for a maximum of 6 hours.
